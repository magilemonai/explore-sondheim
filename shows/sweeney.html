The lyrical lens

Gallows wit, at a sprint

Sondheim's words here are a black comedy of manners. The patter rhymes at full tilt — internal rhymes nested inside internal rhymes — while the subject underneath is revenge, murder, and worse. The horror lands because the wit never once breaks character.

Its peak is “A Little Priest”: an entire genteel waltz spun from a single ghastly pun, every fresh victim folded politely into the menu. An interactive patter-room for it opens once those bars are transcribed.
